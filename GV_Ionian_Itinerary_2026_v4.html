Phase 1

Croatia outbound

16–18 Jun · 3 days

Three days south through Dalmatia, ending at the staging anchorage for the Corfu crossing. Keep it easy — the point is to arrive rested, not to cram in sightseeing.

Dubrovnik is tempting but it asks too much of this day. Cavtat is the professional choice: easier berth, better departure position, quieter night before the big passage.

Phase 2

The Corfu crossing

19–20 Jun · offshore
⚠️

This is the hinge of the trip

The Cavtat–Corfu passage (~155 nm) is the biggest decision of the voyage. Everything else in the schedule should serve this leg — not the other way around. Depart only on a genuinely good 36-hour forecast. A one-day delay is far cheaper than a rough crossing.

  • Check VHF NWS + Windy 48h before departure
  • Brief the crew on watch rotation before leaving the dock
  • Albanian territorial waters: stay 12+ nm off the coast if you transit at night
  • Preferred arrival: Gouvia marina (practical, well-staffed). Old Port if the weather is perfect and you have time.

155 nm ⚓ Crossing
Before departure
  • Sleep in the morning, rest in the afternoon
  • Fuel, water, food, safety gear check
  • Clear Croatian customs/border
  • Confirm forecast: leave only if it's genuinely comfortable
Skipper rule

If there is any doubt about the forecast, wait. The schedule has buffer — the Adriatic does not forgive rushing.

Arrival day is recovery day. Keep it simple.
🏛 Marina
Arrival
  • Gouvia marina: practical, reliable, has everything you need
  • Check into Greece (transit log/crew list)
  • No plans for the evening — just eat and sleep
Skipper note

Old Port is more beautiful but gets crowded and has awkward stern-to berthing. After an overnight passage, Gouvia is the right call.

Phase 3

Ionian — solo days

21–22 Jun · 2 days

Two days to decompress from the crossing and position the boat for guest pickup near Preveza/Lefkada airport. Keep it gentle.

The Ionian — turquoise water, steady breeze, easy anchoring
6
Sat 21 Jun
Corfu → Paxos / Lakka
Classic easy Ionian day. Beautiful anchorage, no pressure.
28 nm ⚓ Anchor
Plan
  • Late morning departure — no rush
  • Stop at Antipaxos for a swim if the weather is settled
  • Anchor Lakka (prettier) or Gaios (livelier) based on mood

Paxos fills up fast in summer. Arrive by mid-afternoon for a good spot. Lakka has a tighter entrance — take it slow.

7

Paxos → Lefkada / Preveza area
Positioning leg before guest pickup. Practicality over romance today.
45 nm ⚓ Anchor Positioning
Plan
  • Early start to cover the miles comfortably
  • Optional stop at Sivota only if time allows
  • Anchor Dessimi Bay or Vlicho — calm, easy, good for boat prep
Skipper note

Tomorrow guests arrive. Use the afternoon to provision, clean the boat, and get a proper night's sleep. Nydri is convenient for shops but noisy — Dessimi is better for rest.

Phase 4

Guest week

23–30 Jun · 8 days

The heart of the trip. Short legs, beautiful spots, good evenings ashore. The Ionian does this better than anywhere in the Med — don't waste it on long transfer days.

Guest week — Meganisi, Fiskardo, Ithaka, Porto Katsiki
8
Mon 23 Jun · guest arrival
Guests arrive via Preveza/Aktio airport
Keep this day completely easy — no sailing, just welcome and settle in.
👥 Guests arrive
Plan
  • Pick up guests from Preveza airport (taxi or dinghy to dock)
  • Short orientation sail or sunset cruise if guests are keen
  • Dinner ashore in Nydri or on the boat
Skipper note

Don't try to cover miles today. First day with guests should feel effortless — save the impressive sailing for when they've settled in.

The Ionian at its best: crystalline water, dramatic scenery, superb anchorages.
15–22 nm/day 👥 With guests ⚓ Mixed
Suggested stops
  • Spartochori / Vathy, Meganisi — tiny island, stunning water
  • Fiskardo, Kefalonia — the prettiest harbour town in the Ionian
  • Swim stops at Ithaka channel if conditions allow
Skipper note

Fiskardo stern-to on the quay is the classic Ionian experience. Book a table at one of the waterfront restaurants — they fill up fast in late June.

11–12
Thu–Fri 26–27 Jun
Fiskardo → Ithaka (Kioni / Vathy) → Atokos
Ithaka is one of the most rewarding stops in the Ionian — Homer's island, worth an overnight.
12–20 nm/day 👥 With guests ⚓ Anchor
Suggested stops
  • Kioni or Vathy, Ithaka — quiet, beautiful, great tavernas
  • Atokos island — uninhabited, phenomenal anchorage, worth the detour
  • Swim day at Atokos — one of the best in the Ionian
Skipper note

Atokos: anchor in the main bay, use a stern line if it's busy. Water clarity is exceptional. Don't skip it.

13–14
Sat–Sun 28–29 Jun
Atokos → Meganisi → Porto Katsiki (west Lefkada)
Porto Katsiki is the showstopper of the trip — save it for near the end of the guest week.
20–30 nm/day 👥 With guests ⚓ Anchor
Suggested stops

  • Porto Katsiki: anchor close to the white cliffs — unforgettable
  • Egremni (adjacent bay) if Porto Katsiki is too crowded

Porto Katsiki gets crowded in the afternoon with day boats. Arrive early (before 10am) or stay for sunset when they all leave — both work beautifully.

Return crossing & Croatia

45 + 28 nm ⚓ / 🏛 Staging

Skipper note

The meltemi builds in July — check if there's any NW fetch developing. An earlier departure (before it builds) is often better than waiting for perfect conditions that may not come.

This section is already the best part of the original plan. Keep it flexible — if you're tired, shorter legs. If you're energised, push a bit further. No pressure: you're almost home.
