Good Vibrations · Ionian itinerary review

Mid-June Greece trip — cleaned up.

The old version had charm, but it read like a travel brochure. This version is more useful: clearer pacing, fewer overloaded days, more emphasis on the two offshore crossings, and a cleaner split between scenic days, positioning days, and guest-friendly days.

What to improve

My view on the current itinerary

  • Too much promotional language; not enough operational clarity.
  • Dubrovnik is treated as both a major sightseeing stop and a passage staging stop — that’s asking too much.
  • The offshore legs should dominate the schedule logic; everything around them should stay flexible.
  • The guest week is broadly good, but some days should stay deliberately short and easy.
  • The return section is actually one of the stronger parts and mostly just needs cleaner presentation.

What I changed

Content direction

less fluffclearer choicesbetter pacingmore respect for weather

  • Separated beautiful days from positioning days.
  • Made Cavtat / Corfu crossings the real anchors of the plan.
  • Kept guest legs mostly in the 15–25 nm sweet spot.
  • Shortened every day into the bits that actually matter onboard.

Recommended structure

Route logic

If I were tightening this trip for real use, I’d think of it in four blocks: reach Cavtat in good shape, cross cleanly to Corfu, enjoy a guest-friendly Ionian loop, then set up and execute the return crossing without schedule pressure. The crossings are the hard edges. The rest should stay supple.

Daily plan

Condensed itinerary

Day 1 · Mon 16 Jun · Outbound

Vrboska → Korčula Town

30 nm · Harbour

A sensible first leg: enough miles to settle in, easy reward at the end.

Plan
  • Early departure from Vrboska
  • Optional swim stop near Badija / Lumbarda side if conditions are easy
  • Arrive Korčula with time to walk the old town
Skipper note

Good first-night harbour. Book ahead if you want certainty.

Day 2 · Tue 17 Jun · Outbound

Korčula → Polače, Mljet

25 nm · Anchor

Moves the boat south without overreaching, and gives you a quiet anchorage before Dubrovnik.

Plan
  • Sail via Mljet channel
  • Optional lunch / swim at Okuklje only if it does not slow the day too much
  • Anchor Polače or switch to Pomena if weather / park access suits better
Skipper note

Polače is better for a quiet night; Pomena is better if you want more shore life.

Day 3 · Wed 18 Jun · Outbound

Mljet → Dubrovnik / Cavtat area

35 nm · Harbour

This should be treated as a staging day, not a sightseeing marathon.

Plan
  • Aim to reach your departure staging area in good time
  • Dubrovnik only if berth is secured and you really want the city stop
  • Otherwise go straight to Cavtat and keep the boat rested for the crossing
Skipper note

I would lean toward Cavtat over Dubrovnik if the overnight passage is the priority.

Day 4 · Thu 19 Jun · Passage setup

Cavtat → overnight departure for Corfu

155 nm · At sea

This is the hinge of the trip. The whole schedule should serve this leg, not the other way around.

Plan
  • Sleep/rest in the afternoon
  • Fuel, food, weather, watch system, paperwork checked before departure
  • Leave only if the 24–36h forecast is genuinely comfortable
Skipper note

If the weather is mediocre, delay. For this trip, discipline beats schedule.

Day 5 · Fri 20 Jun · Passage / arrival

Offshore → Corfu

Arrival day · Harbour

Arrival day should be light. No heroics after the crossing.

Plan
  • Enter calmly, check in, recover
  • Prefer marina / easy berth over a romantic but awkward arrival
  • Keep evening simple
Skipper note

Gouvia is the practical option. Old Port is prettier but not necessarily worth the hassle after an overnight.

Day 6 · Sat 21 Jun · Ionian southbound

Corfu → Paxos / Lakka

28 nm · Anchor

Classic gentle Ionian day after arrival recovery.

Plan
  • If weather is settled, stop at Antipaxos briefly
  • Avoid forcing both a long beach stop and late anchoring
  • Sleep in Lakka or Gaios depending wind direction and mood
Skipper note

Lakka is prettier; Gaios is livelier.

Day 7 · Sun 22 Jun · Positioning

Paxos → Lefkada side / Preveza area

45 nm · Anchor

This is a positioning leg before guest pickup, so simplicity matters more than sightseeing.

Plan
  • Leave early
  • Use Sivota stop only if it does not create a late arrival
  • Finish somewhere practical for airport access and boat prep
Skipper note

Dessimi / Vlicho are practical. Choose practicality over romance here.

Day 8–15 · 23–30 Jun · With guests

Preveza / Lefkada / Meganisi / Fiskardo / Ithaka loop

Moderate daily legs · Mixed

This week should prioritise easy beauty, swimming, and good evenings ashore — not distance.

Plan
  • Keep most legs 15–25 nm
  • Build around Meganisi, Fiskardo, Ithaka, Atokos, Porto Katsiki
  • Avoid overloading days with both major sightseeing and long transfers
Skipper note

The strongest guest section is Meganisi → Fiskardo → Ithaka → Atokos / Meganisi → west Lefkada.

Day 16–17 · 1–2 Jul · Return setup

Preveza → Paxos → Corfu

45 nm + 28 nm · Anchor / Harbour

This sets up the return crossing. Keep it efficient.

Plan
  • Use Paxos as a good stop, not an overcomplicated sightseeing day
  • Be in Corfu early enough to reset the boat and crew
  • Again: do not force departure if forecast is poor
Skipper note

Same rule as outbound — the crossing decides the schedule.

Day 18–21 · 3–6 Jul · Croatia return

Dubrovnik area → Lopud / Mljet / Korčula / Vrboska

Manageable coastal legs · Mostly anchor

Nice decompression after the second crossing. This section is already broadly right.

Plan
  • Prioritise rest after landfall
  • Then short beautiful legs back north
  • Keep one weather buffer in your head in case the return crossing slips
Skipper note

Good section overall; only keep it flexible around the offshore arrival day.

What I would watch

Operational pressure points

  • Dubrovnik vs Cavtat: don’t try to make that day do everything.
  • Outbound crossing: leave only on a genuinely easy forecast.
  • Guest pickup week: keep transfer days simpler than they look on paper.
  • Return crossing: preserve schedule slack so Croatia arrival is not forced.

Looks

Design changes I’d carry through

  • Turn it into a clean web page with a map panel and a compact day-by-day rail.
  • Use fewer photos and more structure.
  • Add clear tags: anchor / harbour / crossing / positioning / guest day.
  • Add one printable PDF version afterwards, not as the primary experience.

Bottom line

The trip itself is good. The presentation needed discipline.

The best change is not inventing more destinations — it’s making the itinerary calmer, more legible, and more honest about what matters: the two offshore crossings, guest comfort, and avoiding rushed setup days.
